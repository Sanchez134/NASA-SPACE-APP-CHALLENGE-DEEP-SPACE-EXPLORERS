Unsustainable energy is energy which is obtained from non-renewable resources. This type of energy encompasses the majority of energy used currently. Non-renewable resources are finite, meaning that they will deplete after a certain amount of time, hence the title unsustainable.
Examples of unsustainable energy:
Coal
Gas
Oil
Nuclear Energy.
Most of your energy is likely produced through the combustion of these resources!

As stated before, non-renewable energy will deplete in the future. Fossil fuels, such as coal and natural gas, are estimated to be completely exhausted by 2060 if we continue to consume them at the current rate.
In addition to this, most unsustainable energy sources result in harm to the ecosystem. As the usage of fossil fuels includes combustion, greenhouse gases are released into the atmosphere. Greenhouse gases are gaseous pollutants which cause heat to be trapped in the atmosphere. This leads to global temperatures increasing, called global warming.

Climate change represents the transformation of a climate on Earth. Climate differs from weather in that climate describes long term atmospheric conditions, usually over 30 years or more, whereas weather refers to shorter periods of time, such as throughout the day. Climate change occurs rapidly throughout the progression of modern society, through the combustion of fossil fuels and other human activities. This leads to unnatural temperature increases, known as global warming. Global warming represents the temperature increase specifically, while climate change is associated with differing climates over time, meaning that both temps have differing meanings.

Immediate Economic Benefits: Fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas are cheap to extract and abundant. Despite their environmental impact, they remain attractive due to their low cost (U.S. Energy Information Administration).
Infrastructure Dependence: Many countries rely on infrastructures powered by fossil fuels. Shifting to renewable energy would require significant investments and technological changes, making the transition difficult for some nations.
Political and Corporate Interests: Fossil fuel industries hold strong political influence. Oil and gas companies often lobby for legislation that protects their interests and prevents the development of renewable energy.
Lack of Awareness: Some leaders deny or downplay the long-term effects of fossil fuel dependence, delaying necessary actions against climate change and environmental degradation.

Investing in Renewable Energy: Similarly, the International Renewable Energy Agency, IRENA also reports the ability to reduce dependency on fossil fuel consumption through integrating solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal renewable energies that offer clean and affordable electricity supplies.
Improve Energy Efficiency: According to EPA, improvement in energy efficiency in industries, homes, and transport would result in a large reduction in overall consumption of energy. Smart grids, efficient appliances, and eco-friendly buildings are the important constituents of this strategy.
Support Clean Energy Innovations: Government and private industries can invest in the development of new technologies that have to do with advanced batteries, and carbon capture, among others that would hasten the transition to renewable energy.
Carbon Pricing: There is the need to implement carbon taxes or cap-and-trade schemes so that fossil fuels become less economical to produce, hence compelling companies to start switching to cleaner forms of energy.(World Bank, 2021)
Government and Corporate Involvement: While governments need to set up more stringent environmental regulations, companies should also try to find less harmful ways to do business as usual to reduce environmental impact.(Greenpeace, 2021)
Public Awareness: It educates people to be personally involved in green energy and sustainable living by spreading the knowledge of benefits arising therefrom. These conscious choices will have a positive change on the environment.(UNEP, 2020)
International Cooperation: Wide environmental protection and sustainable development may be pursued through international cooperation by agreements such as the Paris Climate Accord.

Everyday tasks:
Turn off electronics when not in use.
Activate low power mode on appliances.
Use energy-efficient appliances such as fluorescent lamps.
Consume seasonal produce.
Purchase groceries in bulk.
Advocacy:
Provide advice to close friends and family.
Advertise programmes and foundations supporting sustainable energy usage.
Donations:
Donate to foundations which support clean energy.
